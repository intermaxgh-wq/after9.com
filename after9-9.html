About

Accra, Ghana. Founded by people who kept designing after the day job ended.

AFTER9 started as a name in a group chat — a joke about the only hours we had free to actually make anything. Full-time jobs by day, sketching and cutting patterns by night. The name stuck because it was true: everything we make is built after 9.

We work with tailors across Accra, cut in small batches, and drop when a piece is ready rather than on a fashion calendar. No overproduction, no seasons — just gear made for people who are still out when everyone else has gone home.
